Where to get the COVID-19 vaccine in South Florida

7 News WSVN

(WSVN) - Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis has opened the distribution of vaccines to members of the general population who are 65 years or older, police officers and firefighters 50 years of age and all teachers.

Several hospitals and sites require appointments in order for eligible South Floridians to receive the vaccine.

NOTE: Health officials warn that “appointments are limited at this time. More appointments will be made available as vaccine supply increases and additional sites open.”

Federally Run Site

Miami-Dade College North: A federal vaccination site has opened at the Miami-Dade College North Campus. The walk-up site will open from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. daily and will administer 2,000 vaccines daily without appointments. For more information, click here.

Statewide Programs

MyVaccine Florida – The state has developed a pre-registration system to schedule vaccinations for priority populations. Click here to pre-register.

Walmart | Sam’s Club will be allowing eligible Floridians to receive the COVID-19 vaccine at select Walmart and Sam’s Club stores through the new Federal Retail Pharmacy Program. Click here for participating Walmart and Sam’s Club locations.

Broward County

Broward Health will begin COVID-19 vaccinations at its new community stand-up site at Inter Miami CF Stadium at Lockhart Park. The site will be open Monday through Friday and expects to vaccinate up to 500 pre-registered patients a day. To register, click here.

FDOH Broward: The Florida Department of Health in Broward is providing vaccination appointments to people who meet the requirements at various sites across the county. For more information or to view the vaccination sites, click here. To make an appointment, click here.

Memorial Healthcare System: Memorial Healthcare System is offering vaccines to those who meet the requirements, by appointment only. Appointments can be scheduled through MyChart if you are an existing patient. If you are a new patient, please call 954-276-4340 to schedule your COVID-19 vaccine. For more information, click here.

Publix Supermarket: Starting Friday, February 26 after 7:00 a.m. EST, eligible customers can access COVID-19 vaccine appointment availability beginning Monday, March 1.  For more information, click here.

Broward College North Campus in Coconut Creek: Hosts a state-run vaccination site. This location will accept walk-ups and those who have proactively scheduled appointments.  Click here for more.

Coral Square Mall: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Tradewinds Park: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Markham Park: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Tree Tops Park: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Snyder Park: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Central Broward Regional Park: Individuals 65+, long-term facility residents and staff, healthcare personnel with direct patient contact and individuals deemed to be extremely vulnerable to COVID-19 by hospital providers. Click here for more.

Holy Cross Health: Those under age 65 who meet the requirements can request an appointment at the hospital. Priority will be given to Holy Cross Medical Group patients.

Collins Community Center: The City of Oakland Park is offering free vaccines to seniors 65+ by appointment only at the Collins Community Center at 3900 NE Third Avenue. To make an appointment, call 954-630-4335, Monday to Friday, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Appointments are available Monday to Saturday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. You do not have to be a resident of Oakland Park.

Miami-Dade County

County Vaccination Program: Miami-Dade County is coordinating closely with hospital and healthcare partners, the Florida Department of Health, municipalities, and private partners to vaccinate members of the community. For more information, click here. To book an appointment, click here.

Jackson Health System is offering vaccines to those 65 years old and older. Certain adults 55 and older with specific medical conditions are now able to schedule a COVID-19 vaccination. To make an appointment, click here.

Mt. Sinai: First Responders and seniors over 65 can schedule an appointment by calling 305-674-2312, Monday-Friday from 8 a.m. – 5 p.m. and Saturday – Sunday from 8 a.m. – 1 p.m. No walk-ins will be accepted.

Hard Rock Stadium: Seniors 65 and older are able to call 1-888-499-0840 to make an appointment to get vaccinated at the Hard Rock Stadium. The stadium currently offers 1,000 appointments available per day and additional appointments are dependent on additional vaccine doses allocated by the federal government.

Baptist Health: Baptist Health South Florida will begin offering COVID-19 vaccines to senior residents and people who are at high risk of serious illness from the virus, beginning Monday, Jan. 11. The hospital is only offering a limited number of appointments and will offer more as they receive more vaccines. For more information, click here.

Jessie Trice Community Health System: Seniors 65 and older are able to call 305-637-6400 and make an appointment to get vaccinated. Testing will be conducted Wednesday through Friday from 8:30 a.m. to 4 p.m.

Miami VA:  Miami VA Healthcare System is offering COVID-19 Vaccines to all eligible Veterans, regardless of age, in Miami-Dade, Broward and Monroe counties who are active patients of the Miami VA Healthcare System. To schedule an appointment, Veterans can call 305-575-7000 or contact their care team.

Navarro Discount Pharmacies: Seniors 65+ can schedule COVID-19 vaccines at all their Miami-Dade County locations.

CVS y Mas: Seniors 65+ can schedule COVID-19 vaccines at all their Miami-Dade County locations. For more information, click here.

Fresco y Mas: The grocery change Fresco y Mas is offering COVID-19 vaccines to eligible recipients at their store located at 18300 SW 137Th Avenue in SW Miami-Dade. For more information click here.

Winn-Dixie: Winn-Dixie’s pharmacy will begin offering COVID-19 vaccines to eligible recipients two Miami-Dade locations: 1155 NW 11th Street and 1150 NW 54th Street in Miami. For appointments and more information click here.

Publix Supermarket: Starting Friday, February 26 after 7:00 a.m. EST, eligible customers can access COVID-19 vaccine appointment availability beginning Monday, March 1.  For more information, click here.

Overtown Youth Center: No appointment is necessary for those who want to get vaccinated at the Overtown Youth Center, located at 450 NW 14th St. The site is open seven days a week from 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Organizers said they are able to administer 200 vaccine doses each day.

Ronselli Park in Sweetwater: Long-term facility residents and staff, persons 65 years of age and older, health care personnel with direct patient contact, police officers, firefighters and school employees 50 years of age and older can get vaccinated at the site. It is open daily from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Walk-ups are allowed and appointments are not necessary. A total of 500 doses will be administered daily.

Monroe County

FDOH Monroe: The Florida Department of Health in Monroe County is providing vaccination appointments to:

  • Long-term care facility residents and staff
  • Persons 65 years of age and older
  • Persons under 65 deemed medically vulnerable
  • Health care personnel with direct patient contact
  • K-12 school employees
  • Sworn law enforcement officers 50 years of age and older
  • Firefighters 50 years of age and older

Call 305-293-7500 to schedule an appointment to be vaccinated at their site in Tavernier.

Winn-Dixie: Winn-Dixie’s pharmacy will begin offering COVID-19 vaccines to eligible recipients at two of their Monroe County locations (Key West and Tavernier). For appointments and more information click here.
